<div style="text-align: left;"><span style="font-family: inherit;">Today's seminar talk is by&nbsp;<span style="caret-color: rgb(0, 0, 0);">Giorgio Genovesi from Leeds, who will&nbsp;</span><span style="caret-color: rgb(0, 0, 0);">be talking about countable second countable topological spaces in the context of reverse mathematics.</span><span class="Apple-converted-space" style="caret-color: rgb(0, 0, 0);">&nbsp;</span></span></div><div style="text-align: left;"><span style="font-family: inherit;"><span class="Apple-converted-space" style="caret-color: rgb(0, 0, 0);"><br /></span><b>Title: </b>Characterizing Regular Countable Second Countable Spaces in Second Order Arithmetic</span></div><div style="text-align: left;"><span style="font-family: inherit;"><br /></span></div><div class="elementToProof" style="text-align: left;"><span style="font-family: inherit;"><b>Abstract: </b>Regular countable second countable (CSC) spaces admit rather nice characterizations and can easily be formalized in second order arithmetic. It is natural to ask what set existence axioms are needed to ensure regular CSC spaces remain nice. It turns out many theorems which characterize regular CSC are equivalent to one of the big five subsystems of second order arithmetic.</span></div><div><br /></div>
