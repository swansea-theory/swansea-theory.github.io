<p style="text-align: left;"><span style="font-size: small;"><span style="font-family: inherit;">Next week <a href="https://research.ugent.be/web/person/giovanni-solda-0/publications/en" target="_blank">Giovanni Solda</a> is visiting us from Ghent. He will give a talk on "<span class="ContentPasted0">A combinatorial principle weak over weak systems yet strong over strong systems</span>".</span></span></p><div class="elementToProof" style="-moz-text-size-adjust: auto; -webkit-text-stroke-width: 0px; color: black; font-style: normal; font-variant-caps: normal; font-weight: 400; letter-spacing: normal; text-align: left; text-decoration: none; text-indent: 0px; text-transform: none; white-space: normal; word-spacing: 0px;"><p><span style="font-size: small;"><span style="font-family: inherit;"><b>Abstract:<span class="Apple-converted-space">&nbsp;</span></b><span class="ContentPasted1">&nbsp;</span></span></span></p><p><i><span style="font-size: small;"><span style="font-family: inherit;"><span class="ContentPasted1">Better quasi orders (henceforth bqos) are a strengthening of the notion of well quasi order. Even if their definition is more complicated, the former enjoy nice closure properties that make them, in a way, easier to work with than the latter: this feature made bqos an&nbsp;instrumental tool in proving landmark results like Nash-Williams'&nbsp;theorem and Laver's theorem. From the reverse mathematical point of&nbsp;view, the study of bqos is an interesting area still full of open&nbsp;questions.<br class="ContentPasted1" />In this talk, we will focus on a property of non-bqos, the so-called&nbsp;minimal bad array lemma, and in particular one version of it that we&nbsp;will call MBA − . We will show that MBA − has a very odd behavior when&nbsp;it comes to its reverse-mathematical strength, namely&nbsp;</span></span></span></i></p><div><p><span style="font-family: inherit; font-size: small;"><span class="ContentPasted1"><i>• over ATR 0 , MBA − can be seen to be equivalent to the very strong principle of Π 12 -comprehension, yet<br class="ContentPasted1" />• over ACA 0 , MBA − does not imply ATR 0.<br class="ContentPasted1" />In order to prove this result, we will provide a characterization of the&nbsp;quasi-orders that ACA 0 proves to be bqos.</i></span></span></p><p><span style="font-family: inherit; font-size: small;"><span class="ContentPasted1"><i>&nbsp;</i><br class="ContentPasted1" />This is joint work with Anton Freund, Alberto Marcone, and Fedor&nbsp;Pakhomov.</span></span></p></div></div>
