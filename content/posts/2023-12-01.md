<span style="font-family: inherit; font-size: small;">We are thrilled to extend a warm welcome to <a href="https://trottadavide.github.io/" target="_blank">Davide Trotta</a>, a
postdoctoral researcher at the Department of Computer Science at the University
of Pisa in Italy. Davide brings his knowledge and expertise to our community,
and we are excited to have him as a guest speaker in our upcoming seminar
series, which will take place on Monday, 4 December in our Theory Lab.</span>

<span style="font-family: inherit; font-size: small;">

</span><span style="font-family: inherit; font-size: small;"><b>Title: </b>Categorifying computable reducibilities</span>

<span style="font-family: inherit; font-size: small;"><b>Abstract: </b>One of the most relevant notions of categorical logic which
enabled the study of logic from a purely algebraic perspective is the notion of
a (hyper)doctrine, introduced in a series of seminal papers by F.W. Lawvere to
synthesize the structural properties of logical systems. In this talk, I will
introduce this categorical approach to logic and explain how various notions
well-known in computability can be framed within this categorical framework.</span>

<span style="font-family: inherit; font-size: small;">In particular, I will present categorical formulations in
terms of Lawvere doctrines for the Medvedev, Muchnik, and Weihrauch
reducibilities.</span>

<span style="font-family: inherit; font-size: small;">Finally, I will discuss some universal properties of such
doctrines, showing how this study enables us to identify, from an abstract
perspective, the common features of these notions of reducibility.</span>

<span style="font-family: inherit; font-size: small;">This talk is based on joint work with Manlio Valenti and
Valeria de Paiva.</span>

<span style="font-family: inherit; font-size: small;">
