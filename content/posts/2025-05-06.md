<span style="font-family: inherit;">This Thursday

</span><a href="https://trottadavide.github.io/" style="font-family: inherit;" target="_blank">Davide Trotta</a><span style="font-family: inherit;">

from the University of Padova will give a talk on "A topos for extended Weihrauch degrees" as a part of our seminar series.</span>

<span style="font-family: inherit; font-size: small;"><b>Abstract:

</b></span><span style="font-family: inherit;">Weihrauch reducibility [2] is a key notion of reducibility between computational problems that is useful to calibrate the uniform computational strength of a multi-valued function. Such reducibility provides a framework where one can formalize questions such as “which theorems can be transformed continuously or computably into another?”</span>

<div class="elementToProof" style="direction: ltr;"><span style="font-family: inherit;">

The main purpose of this talk is to provide a full categorical account of a generalization of such a notion introduced by A. Bauer called extended Weihrauch reducibility [1]. In particular, we present a tripos [3] and a topos for extended Weihrauch degrees. Then we employ the free constructions and tools developed in [4] to provide a formal connection with realizability, showing that the effective topos is a topos of j-sheaves for a certain Lawvere-Tierney topology over the topos of extended Weihrauch degrees.</span></div><div class="elementToProof" style="direction: ltr;"><span style="font-family: inherit;"><br /></span></div><div class="elementToProof" style="direction: ltr;"><span style="font-family: inherit;">This talk is based on joint work with S. Maschio</span></div><div class="elementToProof" style="direction: ltr;"><span style="font-family: inherit;"><br /></span></div><div class="elementToProof" style="direction: ltr;"><span style="font-family: inherit;">[1] Bauer, Andrej, Instance reducibility and Weihrauch degrees, Logical Methods in Computer Science 18 (2022), no. 3, 20:1–20:18</span></div><div class="elementToProof" style="direction: ltr;"><span style="font-family: inherit;">[2] Brattka, Vasco and Gherardi, Guido, Weihrauch degrees, omniscience principles and weak computability, The Journal of Symbolic Logic 76 (2011), no. 1, 143–176</span></div><div class="elementToProof" style="direction: ltr;"><span style="font-family: inherit;">[3] Pitts, A. M., Tripos theory in retrospect, Mathematical Structures in Computer Science 10 (2000), no. 3, 283–306</span></div><div class="elementToProof" style="direction: ltr;"><span style="font-family: inherit;">[4] Maietti, M.E. and Trotta, D., A characterization of generalized existential completions, Annals of Pure and Applied Logic 174 (2023), no. 4, Paper No. 103234</span></div><span style="font-family: inherit; font-size: small;">
